I wish / If only

📌 Rappel grammatical

wish + past simple → regret sur le présent : I wish I had a car. (= je n'en ai pas)
wish + past perfect → regret sur le passé : I wish I had studied harder. (= je n'ai pas assez étudié)
wish + would → agacement / souhait de changement chez quelqu'un d'autre (jamais pour soi-même) : I wish he would stop smoking.
IF007-I-WISH
  1. 1.I don’t eat a lot of vegetables. I wish I ate more vegetables.
  2. 2.I can’t travel to New York. I haven’t got enough money. I wish I had enough money to travel to New York.
  3. 3.They didn’t score a goal. I wish they had scored a goal.
  4. 4.My car is so small. I wish I had a bigger car.
  5. 5.They lost the photo. I wish they hadn’t lost the photo.
  6. 6.I didn’t study hard at school. I wish I had studied harder at school.
  7. 7.My sister invited me to her party, but I didn’t go. I wish I had gone to my sister’s party.
  8. 8.I only have one child, so I’m lonely. I wish I had more children.
  9. 9.I don’t have a fast car. I wish I had a fast car.
  10. 10.They were not quiet last evening. I wish they had been quieter last evening.
  11. 11.The train was late. I wish the train hadn’t been late.
  12. 12.They had an accident because of the bad weather. I wish the weather hadn’t been so bad.
